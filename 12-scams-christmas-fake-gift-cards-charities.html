From fake gift cards to Secret Santa fraudsters, here are the 12 scams to beware of for Christmas

WITH the holidays just around the corner, many people are looking for the perfect gift to give to friends and family - however, experts warn Americans of Christmas scams that could turn their celebrations sour.

The Better Business Bureau (BBB) has outlined in jovial holiday fashion the 12 scams of Christmas that Americans should know about before purchasing goods.

ATTACKS ON SOCIAL MEDIA

The BBB warns of social media ads that claim to support a charity or a free trial that in fact are scams in which victims never receive items they purchase or are charged monthly.

Others receive counterfeit items that is different from the one that was advertised.

This is the most common scam and has recorded the highest number of victims - and is why the BBB says it's best to check the business online at bbb.org.

'PAY IT FORWARD' SCAM

Another social media scam is a "pay it forward" (social media gift exchanges) type of scam in which people are urged to send gift cards or money to strangers in a form of Secret Santa.

Instead, people are tricked into sharing their personal information online and then sending funds to scammers expecting something in return.

ONLINE ACCOUNTS

Americans are warned to question emails saying their accounts are compromised, which could lead to them clicking a suspicious link and filling the scammer's website with personal data like credit card numbers and more.

HOLIDAY-THEMED APPS

Another scam involves holiday apps, some of which are geared towards kids, that contain plenty of advertising that can contain malware.

FAKED MIRRORED WEBSITES

The BBB is warning Americans of another scam this holiday season: faked mirrored websites that look like other businesses you frequent.

These sites trick people into downloading malware and making false purchases while sharing private data.

HOLIDAY "WISH LIST" ITEMS

The BBB is asking holiday shoppers to be mindful of low-prices on luxury items such as designer bags and clothing, jewelry, and electronics.

Some popular holiday items may not be available for shipment if you order through social sites.

SENDING FAKE SHIPPING NOTIFICATIONS

Another scam involves sending fake shipping links to people in the hopes they click on a fishy website and download malware - or more so, miss their packages altogether.

FREE VIRTUAL HOLIDAY EVENTS

Other scams will have a usually free holiday event charge people money after scammers advertise the event online with a cover fee.

Americans are warned of the five following scams in what seems too good to be true deals.

FREE GIFT CARDS

Firstly, Americans should avoid anything that involves giving away personal information for "free gift cards."

CHARITY SCAM

The same goes for when sending funds to charities, where scammers take personal information and make private purchases while claiming to represent a well-known charity.

SEASONAL HOLIDAY JOBS

Heartless scammers are tricking people on the job market to pay to submit applications for holiday jobs.

PET SCAM

The BBB also warns Americans to always see physical things in person - like never buying pet that they had not seen in person, or to avoid buying material objects like luxuery goods and designer clothes from an irreputable vendor.
