Two teenagers die after Boxing Day horror crash that left another injured

TWO teenagers have died after a horrific Boxing Day car crash that left another injured.

The two 18-year-old men were passengers in a grey Ford Fiesta, which collided with a black Vauxhall Corsa on the A14 in Suffolk at about 9.30pm.

William Smedley, of Bury St Edmunds, Suffolk, died at the scene of the collision.

A second 18-year-old was rushed to Addenbrooke's Hospital in Cambridge and later passed away.

A memorial to the teenagers has been held in Bury St Edmunds, reports the East Anglian Daily Times.

The male driver of the Fiesta was taken to hospital with injuries not believed to be life threatening.

The Corsa's 19-year-old female driver was uninjured.

Anyone who witnessed the collision is asked to contact the Suffolk Police Serious Collision Investigation Team on 101.
