    • What makes a good casino welcome bonus for UK players?

    It’s easy to get dazzled by a casino bonus that offers huge sums of money and an enormous pile of free spins, but things aren’t always as they seem. Instead of simply going for the biggest bonus you can find, you should opt instead for the one that offers the best value in terms of a staking requirement. Here’s what makes a great casino bonus:

    • Realistic wagering requirements - Bonus cash is always burdened by wagering requirements. These dictate how many times the amount needs to be turned over (churned) before it becomes real, withdrawable cash. As a general rule, a wagering requirement over 50x is virtually impossible to meet and require a significant lucky streak. Welcome bonuses that come with 35x wagering or less are considered to be the fairest in the industry.
    • Quality game allowances - More often than not, your bonus cash and/or free spins can’t be used site-wide. Instead, the terms and conditions will list specific games your spins can be played on, or exclude particular game titles. If the games have low RTP then your chances of hitting winning spins are significantly diminished. Check out the games you’re allowed to play on, and make sure they’re well known titles from quality providers.
    • Fair game weighting and bet size - Not all games contribute equally to wagering requirements. Slots usually contribute at 100%, but table games generally stand at about 10%. Weighting percentages are always buried in the fine print, so take a look and make sure the weighting fits your playing style. Check out the bet size cap too. If you’re limited to 2p spins then you’re unlikely to ever get anything great out of the bonus.
    • Generous timeframes - Once you activate your casino bonus, you need to get to work turning it over to meet wagering requirements. There’s always a time limit attached, but the expiration date will vary from bonus to bonus. Shore timeframes make it impossible for recreational players to succeed. That’s why you should be looking for bonuses with an expiry of about 30 days.

    For example, a 100% bonus up to £100 with 30x wagering and 30 days expiry is far better than 300% up to £1,000 with 70x wagering and 14 days expiry. One such bonus is the Winomania casino bonus.

    Things to look out for in a casino welcome bonus

    Every bonus offer, welcome or otherwise, comes with a list of terms and conditions. Much as they’re not fun to read, it’s essential that you know exactly what you’re signing up for. Here are some of the key points to take note of before accepting a bonus or any other kind of deposit offer:

    • Time limits - Your bonus will always come with an expiry date. You need to complete wagering within the time specified or the bonus will be cancelled and you’ll lose out on any wins generated with the bonus cash. Ideally, 14 to 30 days are required to give you enough time.
    • Wagering requirements - Rollover requirements are a necessary evil of almost every bonus offer. The key is to make sure they’re fair and achievable. Ideally your wagering requirements are limited to 35x, with a 50x wager being the absolute highest you should accept.
    • Wagering contribution table - Knowing the game weighting or wagering contributions is key to successfully turning over your bonus. As a general rule, slot games contribute at 100%, table games contribute at 10% and all other games at 0% - but this can vary from site to site. Also take note of games that are excluded from your wagering contribution.
    • Match percentage - This one can be a little tricky to work out. Most welcome offers keep it simple with a 100% match percentage. That means you get the full amount you deposited once again in bonus cash. So if you deposit £20 you get another £20. However, deposit match bonuses could also be set at other amounts like 50% and 25%. With a 50% deposit match, if you deposit £20, you’ll get £10 in bonus cash.
    • Maximum bets and winning caps - Most casino bonuses come with limits. These limits cover how high you can set your bets, as well as the maximum amount you can win. These two factors are key indicators to determine the value of the bonus. If your win is capped too low, then it’s not worth the trouble of trying to meet wagering requirements. And if your max bet is also low, it could make it too hard to achieve big enough wins to churn the bonus.
    • Limited games - The games you’re permitted to play with your bonus can mean the difference between success and failure. Older slots tend to pay out in very low amounts, and if your bonus can only be used on lower quality games then it’s probably not worth your time. Look out for bonuses that come on flashy, big name games to really ramp up your chances of success.
