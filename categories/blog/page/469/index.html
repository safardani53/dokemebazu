published on February 28, 2024
A HOSPITAL trust has declared a "critical" black alert as pressure on its A&E reaches breaking point. Nottingham University Hospitals Trust (NUH), which runs the Queen's Medical Centre and City Hospital, issued an OPEL 4 on Monday morning. The alert, which the NHS classifies as a "serious incident", was raised to critical on Wednesday. According to HSJ, the trust was experiencing "exceptional pressure" due to patient flow and discharges. Mark Simmonds, NUH’s clinical director of urgent and emergency care, tweeted on Monday that there were 160 patients in the emergency department and, at one point, 14 ambulances arrived within 15 minutes.
