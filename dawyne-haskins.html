Haskins Sr. is the owner of iRideHaskins.com, whereas Tamara works at New York Life Insurance Company. 

Haskins has a younger sister named Tamia Haskins. She is four years younger than him, and both attended the same high school. Haskins sister is an actress and his best friend.

During childhood, Haskins attended many camps. Later with his father, he worked on drills mainly to avoid pressure in the pocket.

Dwayne Haskins | High School Career

During 9th grade, Haskins and his family moved to Potomac. He joined Bullis High School as Haskins was seeking higher competition.

Haskins would even carry a football in a backpack during their high school days. In practice, Haskins would come early to practice footwork drills with receivers.

Haskins Sr. and sometimes Haskins, mom and sister, would come to the practice facility after 9 pm; for one or two-hour sessions.

During his high school football career for Bullis between 2013 to 2016, Haskins passed for 5,308 yards along with 54 touchdowns. 

Haskins had offers from 38 different colleges. Firstly he chose Maryland, but after Randy Edsall, i.e., Maryland coach, was fired, Haskins committed to Ohio State to play football.

During his first year at Ohio State, Haskins redshirted. Later in his second year, Haskins was named a backup for J.T. Barrett and did not start any matches.

Dwayne Haskins | NFL Career

NFL Draft

During preparation for the NFL draft, Haskins worked out with Quincy Avery, Bullis School quarterback coach. He used to train four days a week for six hours.

Haskins used to practice throwing with footwork drills and the next two hours doing lifting and agility work. 

During the 2019 NFL draft Haskins was selected in the first round by Washington Redskins (Washington Commanders) as the 15th pick.

Even though, jersey number 7 was unofficially retired by Washington in honor of Joe Theismann, Haskins was granted the 7 number after his request.

Haskins made his debut for the team against New York Giants in Week 4. He threw 107 passing yards and three interceptions, but Giants defeated Washington with a score of 24-3.

Later in week 9, Haskins made his first-ever start for Washington in a 24-9 loss against Buffalo Bills. 

In week 12, Haskins got his first win as a starter as Washington defeated Detroit Lions 19-16. 

During the 2020 season, Haskins was named one of the team captains and starters for Washington. 

First Covid19 Protocol Breach

In October, before the match against New York Giants, Haskins broke the league Covid19 protocol for the first time.

He invited a family friend to the team hotel in New York. Later, Washington fined him $4,833 for breaking protocol. As a result, Haskins was benched and remained a backup until week 14.

In a game against the San Francisco 49ers, Haskins got a chance to play for the first time after a protocol breach. He came as a substitute for the injured Alex Smith.

Later, Haskins started against the Seattle Sea Hawks in the next match. Haskins threw for 295 yards, including two interceptions and one touchdown. 

Second Breach 

In December, Haskins attended his girlfriend’s birthday party without wearing a mask. The photograph of the party led to another Covid-19 protocol breach.

Later, Haskins was fined $40,000 and stripped from the captain position for breaking protocols again.

But he started the next match later, being benched in the 4th quarter. He threw 14 passes for 154 yards, including two interceptions.  

After discussing both parties, Washington released Haskins before the end of the second year. 

New Team, Pittsburgh Steelers 

Haskins later signed a contract with another NFL team Pittsburgh Steelers, on January 21, 2021. He was named the team’s third-string quarterback behind Ben Roethlisberger and Mason Rudolph. However, he only got to play one game that season.

Dwayne signed a one-year, restricted free-agent tender by the Steelers on March 16, 2022. However, after a couple of weeks of signing his contract, he suffered a tragic accident leading to his death.

Dwayne Haskins | Death

On April 9, 2022, Haskins was reportedly dead, apparently after being hit by a dump truck while trying to cross Interstate 595 near the Fort Lauderdale, Fla., airport.

The initial report by the Florida Highway Patrol suggested that the quarterback was clubbing all night long and was looking for a gas station while a woman was left waiting in the car.

The patrol team also declared that the blood alcohol content in his body was 2.5 times the legal limit in Florida, and also ketamine was found when his urine was tested.
