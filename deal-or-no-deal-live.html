    • How to play Deal or No Deal live

    If you have watched Deal or No Deal on television, you will already be familiar with the concept of this casino game. However, there are some specific features and rules that you must understand before getting started. The main goal is to predict whether the amount of money in your briefcase is higher or lower than that of the banker. Deal or No Deal Live is broken down into different rounds, outlined below:

    Qualification round 

    In the time given, players must spin a three-reel bank vault in a bid to align the gold sections on at the top of the wheel. You can select your stake and level of difficulty. You will also be given the option of purchasing one or two rings that are immediately aligned to the wheel. This increases the cost per spin by 3x or 9x respectively. Each spin also increases the amount of cash inside the most valuable briefcase by anywhere from 75x to 500x your bet. 

    The Top Up Wheel

    The top-up wheel appears on your screen after the qualifying round. Here, players can increase their possible prize money by adding money to their briefcase by 5x-50x your bet. Select the top-up amount, before spinning the wheel. The more wagered, the higher the multipliers get.

    Game Show Round

    You are now ready to dive into the Deal or No Deal Live studio. Here, 16 sealed briefcases are on display, each containing a different amount. The number inside briefcases, ranging from 1-16, represent the sum of money. Upon a set of briefcases being opened, the banker will call with an offer. This is the amount of money for you to exchange what is in your briefcase. Should you not make a decision before the time runs out, “No Deal” will be selected.
