Eli Manning | Career

Early Career

Manning went to the Isidore Newman School in New Orleans, where he got into both high school football and basketball. He excelled in football and created the record of passing for 7389 yards and 89 touchdowns.

After high school, Manning joined the University of Mississippi, where he joined the team known as ‘Ole Miss Rebels.’

His father, Archie Manning, and his brother Cooper Manning went to the same University, where Archie was considered a legend.

The determined player, Eli, faced the shadow of his father’s success while at college. Their freshman year did not bring much success to Manning, but he started his quarterback career during his sophomore year.

In Manning’s senior year, he led the Rebels to a 10-3 and brought a victory in the SBC Cotton Bowl Classic against Oklahoma State.

Towards the end of the college year, Manning won several awards, including the Maxwell Award, the SEC Most Valuable Player Award, and the Jonny Unitas Golden Arm Award.

Manning had set college records of 10,119 passing yards, 81 touchdown passes, and a passer rating of 137.7. Furthermore, he also was nominated for the 2003 Heisman Trophy but became third in the voting.

Professional Football Career

The football star Eli participated in the 2004 NFL Draft and got first picked by the San Diego Chargers. Just before the draft, Eli and his father Archie had announced that Eli would not play if he got selected by the Chargers due to their concerns, and that’s what happened.

Despite being drafted by the Chargers, Eli got to play for the New York Giants. Manning signed a six-year contract of $45 million with the New York Giants.

Eventually, Eli’s NFL career started playing against the Philadelphia Eagles when he relieved Kurt Warner in the fourth quarter of his first season.

During Manning’s rookie year, he finished the season with 1,043 passing yards, 6 touchdowns, and 9 interceptions. Manning proved his worth in 2005 as the starter of the top five quarterbacks.

In 2008, after four years of his football career, Eli led the Giants to their first Super Bowl appearance in 2001. The Giants outperformed at Super Bowl XLII and beat the unbeatable New England Patriots by 17-14.

In addition to the Giants’ victory in 2008, Manning became the first quarterback to throw a last-minute championship-winning touchdown in the NFL title. For his excellent performance, he won the Most Valuable Player of the Game award.

Again in the 2012 Superbowl XLVI, Eli brought victory to the Giants, beating the Patriots 21-17, which got him the MVP title.

Finally, after 16 years long career, Manning retired from the NFL on January 22, 2020. He ended his last professional season with 1042 passing yards, 6 touchdowns, and 5 interceptions in four games.

On the very occasion of Eli’s retirement, the Giants decided never to have a number 10 again.

Eli Manning | Controversy

Manning is no stranger to controversies. With fame comes praises, and whether you like it or not, some sort of criticism. 

2014 Memorabilia Scheme

Back in 2014, Manning was accused of participating in a fake memorabilia scheme alongside the Giants equipment manager Joe Skiba. Apparently, it was said that Manning, including others, was selling equipment that was used. 

As the case continued, it only came to an end in 2018. According to the sources, they settled the argument and lawsuit with a confidential settlement agreement. To illustrate, the lawsuit was filed by Eric Inselberg, Michael Jakab, and Sean Godown. 

Additionally, their case was against the New York Giants, Eli Manning, John Mara, William Heller, Joseph Skiba, Edward Skiba, and Steiner Sports. Also, the lawsuit claimed that they would store used jerseys for “beat up” to make them look authentic. 

As proof, they had even showcased Manning’s email, which, as explained by the Giant’s lawyers, was taken out of context.

Additionally, during the 2017 interviews, Manning denied the accusations against him. As of now, the trials and lawsuits are over. Therewith, the Giants had stated, “focused on football, the fans, and the future.”

San Diego Chargers and Eli Manning 

Manning commenced his professional career after the San Diego Chargers drafted him in the 2004 NFL Draft as the first overall pick in the first round. However, Manning hasn’t ever played for the team to date that drafted him. 

Altogether not much has been known about why he did so, but Archie Manning later opened up the reasons behind it. The first and foremost reason, as he says, is actually that the Chargers were doing poorly back then. 

Therefore, Archie didn’t want Eli to end up like him; thus, Manning’s agent Tom Condon told them that Eli would sit out for the 2004 season. Apart from it, Archie stated that Eli was more suited for the Giants; therewith, they traded him to the Giants.
