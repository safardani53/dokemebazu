Fans call for Alonso to be banned for 'gouging Maitland-Niles' eyes' in Chelsea loss to Arsenal

MARCOS ALONSO courted controversy after clashing with Arsenal substitute Ainsley Maitland-Niles and appearing to catch his face during Chelsea's loss at the Emirates.

Late on in the Blues' 2-0 defeat the Gunners' the pair came together during a corner and the youngster was left on the floor.

The defender has faced a backlash from supporters, with many calling on the FA to act and retrospectively ban him for his alleged actions.

One labelled the Spaniard's actions "absolutely disgraceful" and felt a ban was "imperative."

Another felt the FA left themselves open to criticism if they did not take action for the alleged offence.

The incident capped a bad day for Chelsea, with goals from Alexandre Lacazette and Laurent Koscielny condemning them to defeat.

It closes the gap between fourth-placed Chelsea and fifth-placed Arsenal to just three points, with sixth-placed Manchester United also in close pursuit.

In May 2016 Tottenham midfielder Mousa Dembele was handed a six-game ban for violent conduct after appearing to gouge Diego Costa's eyes.
That incident happened in a fiery clash between Chelsea and Spurs at the end of the 2016 title race.
