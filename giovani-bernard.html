NFL Draft and Contract

On December 14, 2012, Bernard had announced that he was entering the 2013 NFL Draft.

Likewise, the Cincinnati Bengals picked the rising star in the second round. Not to mention, Gio was ranked the fourth-best running back by the draft scouts. 

Professional Career 

Bernard started the rookie season as a back-up to veteran Benjarvus Green-Ellis for the Cincinnati team and started transitioning to a change-of-pace back throughout the whole season.

Stepping up, he played 16 games for the 2015 season, where he finished 730 rushing yards and two rushing touchdowns.

Besides that, the NFL star signed a three-year contract extension with the Cincinnati Bengals on June 8, 2016.

Following that, he also made a record of career-high nine passes for 100 yards, the sixth receiving touchdown of his career against the Pittsburgh Steelers in the 2016 season. 

Likewise, he was also knocked out on a helmet-helmet hit by Pittsburgh Steelers linebacker Ryan Shazier after catching a pass late in the third quarter.

Bengals running back Benard finished his 2017 season with 105 attempts for 458 rushing yards, two rushing touchdowns, 43 receptions for 389 receiving yards, and two touchdowns.

Furthermore, he played 12 games and finished the 2018 season with a good score.

On September 3, 2019,  Bernard had signed a two-year contract extension with the Bengals.

Similarly, the talented running back led the team to a 31–20 victory against the Tennessee Titans in the 2020 season.

While playing for the Titans, he suffered from a hip injury, due to which he was placed on the reserved list on December 14, 2021.

However, he returned to the team on January 15, 2022, and played against the Eagles.
