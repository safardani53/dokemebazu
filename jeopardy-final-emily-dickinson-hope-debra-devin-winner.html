Brutal Final Jeopardy! twist leaves new winner with less money than runner-up in 'unbelievable' fini

JEOPARDY! scored a new champion on Wednesday's episode but in a way that left viewers dumbfounded.

All three contestants overbet on a triple stumper Final Jeopardy, leaving Devin Lohman a 1-day winner with less money than the 2nd place consolation prize.

1-day champ Deb Bilodeau faced Ben Spilsbury, a sales engineer from Long Island City, New York, and Devin, an architectural designer and Master's student from Peachtree City, Georgia.

Deb, a winemaker from Oakland, California had some Jeopardy! fans rooting for them after their memorable $16,000 debut in the last episode.

The grape-crusher with an outpouring of personality responded to a Virgo clue with a hilariously unsavory facial expression.

In their second outing, Deb was leading after the first round with $7,600, but Devin found both Daily Doubles in Double Jeopardy and cashed in on them.

He quickly moved from a distant second to the lead, slightly edging out Deb going into Final Jeopardy.

"What a duel between Deb and Devin," Ken Jennings, 48, remarked before the last commercial break.

Going into Final Jeopardy: Devin had $20,600, Deb just behind at $18,800, and Ben $4,400.

Final Jeopardy under the category "Lives of The Poets" read: "At a seminary that classified students’ degree of faith, Emily Dickinson was 'without' this, which she compares to a bird in a poem."

SCORES & JAWS ALL DROP

First, Ken turned to Ben in third place who said he “wrote something at the last minute.”

Ben wrote “What is: Faith” and revealed he wagered $4399, dropping to $1.

Next, Ken turned to Deb who incorrectly wrote "What is: Doubt" and, in a jarring sight, had wagered their entire $18,800, dropping to $0.

"Oh, without a doubt you wagered all of it,” Ken joked but sounded shocked himself.

Finally, Ken turned to Devin who incorrectly wrote “What is: God."

Ken explained: “No the seminary at Mount Holyoke said that Emily [Dickenson] was without ‘Hope.’ ‘Hope is the thing with feathers,’ she wrote.”

Devin also wagered nearly his entire earnings: “Woah! A big wager of $19400," Ken said.

"Leaving you with $1200, but today that’s enough!”

Devin was declared the new champion with a 1-day amount of $1200.

Ben’s second-place consolation prize was $2000, and Deb was given a third-place consolation prize of $1000.

As the players congratulated each other and their final earnings showed, Devin had less money than Ben's $2000 despite winning.

'FIRST TIME I'VE SEEN THAT!'

Viewers went absolutely haywire on social media over the triple stumper and ensuing chump change scores.

One fan wrote under a YouTube clip: "Wow, small win on Jeopardy! today. Lowest score. Unbelievable."

Another wrote: "First time I saw that in a long freaking time."

"Second place made more than the winner," wrote a third person as a fourth replied: "LOL. That’s right!"

A fifth fan on Reddit wrote: "That was some poor Final Jeopardy wagering," as another said: "Some risky wagers that didn't quite pan out."

A sixth wrote: "Deb could've wagered at most 9,999 and Devin could have gone significantly shorter"

Another agreed that even though it made for a memorable moment, the players all overbet, especially Deb from second place.

"I feel like both Deb and Ben bet sub-optimally. Deb should have bet just enough to get $1 over Devin's score and Ben should have just bet $0 since it likely wouldn't have changed anything anyways. Had either of them done that, Devin wouldn't have won."

On Twitter, someone wrote: “Man, what was the lowest amount someone won with? $1,200 is the lowest I’ve seen in a while.”
