Sir Paul McCartney and Keith Richards discussed the idea of blow-up kennels while high on their holi

SIR Paul McCartney and Keith Richards have discussed a joint venture — making blow-up kennels.

The Beatles legend revealed he and the Rolling Stones guitarist came up with the idea while high on holiday.

Sir Paul, 76, said: “We were designing portable dog kennels that were inflatable. It was a pretty good idea, we thought.

“We got round to, if you had one, you could custom-make them for dalmatians to be polka dot.”

Asked if weed was involved in that idea, he replied: “In the kennels? In the creative process, it was required.”

But he said they had yet to set the wheels in motion for their business idea, which came during a trip to the Turks and Caicos Islands.

Sir Paul said he would meet Keith for lunch most days on private isle Parrot Cay, where they both own luxury villas.

He also rubbished rumours there was ever any rivalry between them. Speaking on comedian Marc Maron’s WTF podcast, Macca said: “The legend grew that there was a rivalry and everything, but it was the opposite.
