Keon Johnson Bio: Net Worth &amp; Family [2023 Update]

Keon Johnson is a professional American basketball player for the Portland Trail Blazers of the National Basketball Association (NBA). 

Imagine being able to do what you were passionate about from a young age. NBA player Keon Johnson is among the tiny fraction of the population who gets to experience what this is like.

He was traded to the Portland Trail Blazers in 2022 after playing a season with Los Angeles Clippers. 

Accordingly, in October 2022, Keon signed an option contract with the Blazers for the 2023-24 season, enabling him to earn $2.81 million.

A native of Shelbyville, Tennessee, the shooting guard attended the University of Tennessee-Knoxville as a freshman only before declaring for the NBA draft.

Johnson caught interest in basketball at a young age; it comes as natural considering his mother played basketball during her high school & college days.

Ahead of the 2022 NBA Summer League, Keon faced a tragedy with the death of his father, whom he considered his mentor & his best friend.

It was tough for him to process the loss, but he now continues to strive for NBA success which was his old man’s dream.

The below article will reveal everything you need to know about Keon Johnson, including his family, relationship & net worth.

Who Are Keon Johnson’s Parents? More On Family!

Born on March 10, 2002, in Shelbyville, Tennessee, Christopher Keon Johnson is one of three children to Bryant Keith Johnson and Conswella Sparrow Johnson.

His parents married on December 26, 2001.

The NBA player has two younger siblings: brother Kegan (b. April 2010) and sister Kora (b. August 2008).

As reported, Keon also has two half-siblings; Breahnna Kaylin C. Johnson and Keith Smith.

Johnson’s mother, Conswella, is a former basketball player who played for Auburn and was selected two times for the All-Southeastern Conference.

Moreover, during high school, she won Class AAA Tennessee Miss Basketball player two times.

Conswella works as a loan documentation officer at First Community Bank Of Tennessee (November 2003-Present).

The Blazers’ shooting guard’s late father served in the United States Marine Corps for 5 years before he pursued a career as a barber. Sadly, in 2022, he died due to a heart attack.

Death Of Father

Keon’s father, Keith Johnson, died on June 19, 2022, at the age of 49.

He had gone jogging around the neighborhood in Shelbyville when he had a heart attack. Keith was rushed to Vanderbilt Bedford Hospital but was pronounced dead later.

On December 7, 2022, Johnson made a heartfelt Instagram post on the occasion of his father’s posthumous 50th birthday & wrote, “Happy Heavenly Birthday, Dad,” followed by an overwhelming caption dedicated to him.

After his dad’s demise, the NBA star told in interviews that his father was like his best friend, who not only inspired him but also mentored him throughout his basketball journey.

An Alumnus Of The University Of Tennessee

Inspired by his mother’s basketball journey, Keon’s basketball passion got a shaping after attending the Webb School in Bell Buckle, Tennessee.

While in junior high school season, Johnson led the team to the Division II-A state semifinals. His senior year stopped after he suffered a season-ending meniscus injury.

By the end of his high school career, he was a five-star prospect & was the highest-ranked player in Tennessee for the 2020 class.

Over the offers from Virginia, Vanderbilt, Florida & Auburn, he committed to the University of Tennessee in August 2019 & enrolled the following year.

As a freshman in 2020, Johnson averaged 11.3 points, 3.5 rebounds, and 2.5 assists per game. After that, on April 7, 2021, he declared for the NBA Draft forgoing college eligibility years.

NBA Career With Knicks & Blazers

On July 29, 2021, Keon Johnson became the 21st overall pick of the New York Knicks in the 2021 NBA Draft.

But he subsequently was traded to LA Clippers in exchange for 2nd round pick of the 2024 NBA Draft & Quentin Grimes.

With the Clippers, Johnson played for only one season (2021-22 season), appearing in only 15 games, averaging 3.5 points, 1.4 rebounds & 0.9 assists per game.

On February 4, 2022, during the mid-season, he was again traded to Portland Trail Blazers alongside Justise Winslow and Eric Bledsoe while acquiring Norman Powell and Rober Covington.

Johnson completed the 2021-22 NBA season with the Blazers, scoring 214 points in 22 games, averaging 9.7 points, 2.7 rebounds & 2.9 assists per game.

Keon Johnson’s Net Worth & Salary

In his early 20s, Keon Johnson enjoys millionaire perks. The NBA player has been a professional since 2021 & has garnered impressive wealth through his basketball career contracts and few sponsorships. 

As of November 2023, the Portland Trail Blazers player has a whopping net worth of $1-$3 million.

On August 6, 2021, he signed a four-year $12.52 million rookie contract with the Los Angeles Clippers (LAC) that averaged a salary of $2.5 million annually.

But halfway through the 2021-22 NBA season, Keon was traded to Portland Blazers on February 4, 2022, thus buying the rest of his contract.

He made $2.183 in the 2021-22 season, earning $953K from the Clippers & the rest of $1.599 million from the Blazers.

Keon Johnson’s 2022-23 season salary was $2.68 million. With two years remaining on his four-year rookie contract, he exercised an option deal with the Blazers on October 6, 2022.

As a result, for the 2023-24 season, Johnson’s payroll is $2.81 million.
