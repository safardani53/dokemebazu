Patient gets bumper 22,000 payout after three separate dentists failed to spot his gum disease - f

A PATIENT whose severe gum disease was missed for 12 years by three dentists has won £22,000 compensation.

Craig Gaunt, 44, switched practices after one of them said he needed fewer check-ups.

A new expert spotted the problem. But Craig still needs extensive treatment and at least three teeth out.

Craig is now set to lose at least three of his teeth after his condition was missed despite regular check-ups at his local NHS practice between 2002 and 2014. One of the dentists even asked him to stop coming so frequently.

Craig then approached dental negligence solicitors who secured the bumper out-of-court settlement - despite none of the dentists admitting liability.

His dental records revealed the three dentists had caused an "unnecessary" and "avoidable" situation in failing to diagnose or treat gum disease.

Craig had been a patient of Dr Henri Wafula between September 2002 and January 2008 at Dental Surgery, in Sutton-in-Ashfield, Notts.

He was then treated by Dr Wafula at The Manor Dental, in Sutton-in-Ashfield, as well as Dr Malgorzata Majewska and Dr Michel Santamarina between August 2008 and July 2014.

The company director, of Sutton-in-Ashfield, Notts, said: “I had six month checks and was told I was fine. Had they done their jobs they’d have seen my disease. I’m angry.”

Drs Henri Wafula, Malgorzata Majewska and Michel Santamarina denied liability but agreed the payout over treatment at two practices.
