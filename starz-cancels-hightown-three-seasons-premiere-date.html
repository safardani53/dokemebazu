Starz cancels fan-favorite crime drama after 3 seasons as it reveals premiere date for 'dark' final

STARZ has canceled a popular crime drama after only three seasons.

The network will air one more season before the show ends for good.

Hightown will end after season 3, according to TVLine.

The outlet exclusively revealed the news on Tuesday.

However, fans don't have to wait that long for a new season, as it premieres January 26.

New episodes will land on the Starz app and streaming/on-demand services on Friday mornings after that date.

Hightown will then be able to be watched on Starz network on Friday nights at 9/8c, starting on January 26.

Jackie Quiñones, played by Monica Raymund, is a National Marine Fisheries Service Agent in Massachusetts.

Her journey to sobriety with drugs and alcohol is overshadowed by a murder investigation that sucks she gets sucked into.

James Badge Dale, Dohn Norwood, Amaury Nolasco, and Atkins Estimond are all set to return.

SEASON THREE SYNOPSIS

TVLine also obtained the plot line for Season 3.

Jackie is “off the wagon and off the force, but that doesn’t stop her from careening into the dark underbelly of picture-perfect Cape Cod to save missing prostitutes and murdered sex workers.”

Ray Abruzzo, played by James, and Alan Santille, played by Dohn, want to take down drug syndicates.

The doors on the state prison keep revolving and Frankie Cuevas, played by Amaury, steps in.

Osito, played by Atkins, comes out, but the drugs are still coming in.

This brings forth Shane Frawley, Fear the Walking Dead‘s Garret Dillahunt, who is a gangster from Boston that wants a cut from the drug trade in Cape Cod.

TVLine ends the synopsis with: “Alliances are made and old loyalties are questioned in an ugly, corrupt place where it will take just an innocent looking babysitter, a stolen bag of drugs and money to kick it wide open.”
