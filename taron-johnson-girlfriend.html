Meg DiMarco | Early life, Family & Education

Her father, a Syracuse University alumnus, is a partner and owner of Summit Automotive, while her mother worked for the Liverpool Central School District.

Meg grew up with her brother Matt.

She went to Liverpool High School and was a girl’s varsity lacrosse team member.

Then, in 2014, she enrolled at Le Moyne College & graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in business management with an entrepreneurship minor in 2018.

NFL Draft & Professional Journey

After an amateur football career at Sheldon High School and Weber State University, Taron Johnson became the round-four (121st overall) pick in the 2018 NFL Draft of the Buffalo Bills.

As a rookie, in the Week 4 game against the Packers, the cornerback recorded his first career sack and forced fumble on Aaron Rodgers.

He made 11 games appearances (starting in two) for 42 tackles (34 solo tackles) in his first season (2018 season).

Johnson’s 2019, 2020 & 2021 seasons concluded with 50 tackles (12 games), 94 tackles (16 games) & 76 tackles (16 games).

In the 2020 Divisional Playoffs, in the 17-3 victory against the Ravens, the NFL player intercepted Lamar Jackson’s pass & returned it for 101 yards. It tied with George Teague’s longest return in NFL playoff history.

In the 2022-23 season, the Buffalo Bills clinched the playoffs as the AFC East division leader, where Taron started in 16 games for 90 combined tackles.
