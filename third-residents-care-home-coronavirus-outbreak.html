Over a third of residents and staff at care home test positive for coronavirus

OVER a third of residents and staff at a care home tested positive for coronavirus.

St Stephen’s in Worcester, reported 33 residents and 18 members of staff with the virus.

⚠️ Read our coronavirus live blog for the latest news & updates

The outbreak was revealed by a relative of a resident who tested positive.

The whistle-blower said: “They have a mass outbreak of coronavirus. I went past today and there does not seem to be many staff as only one car outside.

"I am concerned that they are not doing what is needed to ensure that my relative does not become another fatality.”

The home provides respite care, dementia care and palliative and end of life care.

Dr. Kathryn Cobain, Director for Public Health for Worcestershire said: “Worcestershire County Council is working with Public Health England (PHE) Midlands to support St.Stephen’s care home in Worcester, following an outbreak of Covid-19.

“A total 33 residents and 18 staff have tested positive at the care home and are now following national guidance and self-isolating for ten days."

It is thought one person has needed hospital treatment, so far.

Dr Cobain added: "Close contacts are being identified and told to self-isolate for 14 days, in accordance with the national guidance.

“Public Health partners are working closely with the home to ensure prevention measures are in place and we continue to work collectively to monitor the situation and to ensure all necessary action is taken.”
