Career

Una Healy started her musical career playing the guitar and singing in pubs and clubs around Ireland, both alone and in bands. In 2004, and again in 2006, she won the Glinsk Song Contest in Ireland. At the Eurovision Song Contest in 2006, she performed backup for Brian Kennedy on “Every Song Is a Cry for Love.”

In mid-2006, Una recorded and released her first extended play, Sorry, from which the song “Had It with Today” was re-recorded as the B-side of the Saturdays’ song “Higher,” which she self-produced. Healy relocated to London in 2007 in order to acquire more exposure and experience as a vocalist, and subsequently auditioned for The Saturdays. Healy composed, produced, and performed on the music for Eoin Macken’s film Christian Blake in 2008.

The band released “What About Us” in March 2013, followed by “Gentleman” and “Disco Love” later that year. The radio mix “Not Giving Up” was released in April 2014.
