Who is Jenn Todryk? | The US Sun

JENN Todryk has found fame and major success.

The HGTV host has become an online and television sensation.

Who is Jenn Todryk?

Jenn was born Jennifer Todryk on July 22, 1988, in Burleson, Texas.

She earned a degree in fashion merchandising from Tarleton State University in Stephenville, Texas.

The 34-year-old mom of three is a social media star who is highly popular on Instagram, where she has 1.2million followers.

Jenn is also an author and blogger. As the HGTV website notes, in 2015, she created her blog Life As a Rambling Redhead.

Just seven weeks later, her post titled Top 5 Wines That Pair Best With Your Child's Crappy Behavior became an online hit.

Her book titled W(h)ine: 50 Wines That Pair Perfect With Your Child's Rotten Behavior was published in 2017.

Jenn and her husband Mike Todryk share two daughters — Berkely and Vivienne — and a son, Von.

The home designer often shares photos of her family on her Instagram page, @theramblingredhead.

She also has 191K followers on Facebook, @lifeasaramblingredhead.

In addition to her online success, Jenn has made a name for herself on HGTV as a home décor and renovation specialist.

She also has a podcast with her husband called You, Me & Mike, as well as an Amazon shop under the name The Rambling Redhead.

The shop's description states: "My current Amazon addictions, all in one place!"

Which HGTV show does Jenn Todryk host?

Jenn hosts the TV show No Demo Reno on HGTV.

According to her 'Bio' on the network's website, in the series: "Jenn tears down the notion that major demolition is always necessary and creates beautiful renovations with no demo required."

No Demo Reno first premiered on March 25, 2021, and currently has two seasons.

In addition to viewing through HGTV, fans can watch the show via fuboTV, Discovery Plus, and Philo. It is also available through Hulu with a premium subscription.

Is No Demo Reno coming back to HGTV?

No Demo Reno is returning to HGTV for its third season in the summer of 2023.

According to the HGTV website, there will be 12 new episodes of the successful show.

The site states: "No Demo Reno attracted more than 18.4 million viewers to its sophomore season, making the series a Top 3 non-news/sports cable program among key viewer demographics."
