William Penn Adair Rogers is an American film and theater actor, variety artist, cowboy, comedian, journalist and social commentator from Oklahoma. He is a Cherokee citizen born in the Cherokee Nation, Indian territory. Known as “Oklahoma's Favorite Son,” Rogers was born to a Cherokee family in Indian Territory. As an artist and comedian, he has traveled the world three times, directed 71 films and written over 4,000 national press categories. By the mid-1930s, Rogers was extremely popular in the United States for his greater political spirit and the highest paid star among Hollywood movie stars. He died in 1935 with pilot Wiley Post when their small plane crashed in northern Alaska. Rogers' variety hopping action led to success in Ziegfeld Follies, which in turn led to his first of many film offerings. His appearances as a columnist and radio in the 1920s increased his visibility and popularity.
